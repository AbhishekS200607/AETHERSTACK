Why Your Instagram Page is Not Enough — And What You're Missing

Aetherstack Team · 4 min read

You have 2,000 followers. You post regularly. You get likes and comments.

But when someone wants to seriously hire you — they Google your business name. Nothing comes up. That moment of doubt — "is this business even real?" — costs you the client.

This happens every single day to small businesses across India. And most owners never find out — because the client just quietly moves on to someone who has a website.

Instagram is Rented Land

Your Instagram account doesn't belong to you. It belongs to Meta. They can:

  • Disable your account for any reason, with no warning
  • Change the algorithm so your posts reach 10% of your followers
  • Shut down the platform entirely (remember when Instagram went down for 6 hours in 2021?)
  • Decide tomorrow that business accounts need to pay to be visible

Everything you've built — every follower, every post, every DM conversation — lives on their servers, under their rules. You own none of it.

A website is yours. Your domain, your content, your data. Nobody can take it away.

Google Doesn't Show Instagram in Local Search

This is the part most people don't realize. When someone searches "photographer in Kochi" or "cake shop near me" — Google shows websites. Not Instagram profiles.

Your Instagram posts are not indexed by Google in any meaningful way. A potential customer searching for your service will never find your Instagram through a Google search. They'd have to already know your Instagram handle to find you there.

A website with basic SEO gets indexed by Google within weeks. After that, every search for your service in your city is a chance to be found.

The Clients Who Pay the Most Search Google First

Think about the difference between two types of clients:

Type A scrolls Instagram, sees your post, sends a DM asking "how much?" — often haggles, sometimes ghosts.

Type B has a specific need, searches Google, finds your website, reads about your work, checks your contact page, and sends you a proper inquiry with a budget in mind.

Type B clients are more serious, more prepared, and more likely to pay your actual rate. They found you because they were actively looking. They came to you. That's a completely different dynamic.

Type B clients don't find you on Instagram. They find you on Google.

A ₹1,000 Landing Page Does More Than 1,000 Followers

A single landing page with your name, what you do, your work samples, and a WhatsApp button does three things Instagram can't:

  • It appears in Google search results for your service and location
  • It gives you a professional link you can put on business cards, WhatsApp status, and anywhere else
  • It signals legitimacy — a real business has a real website

1,000 Instagram followers who never buy from you are worth less than 10 Google visitors who are actively searching for what you sell.

Instagram for Discovery. Website for Conversion.

This isn't about choosing one over the other. Instagram is genuinely useful — it builds awareness, shows your personality, and keeps you in front of people who already know you.

But Instagram gets them interested. Your website closes the deal.

When a serious client finds you on Instagram, the first thing they do is Google your business name to verify you're real. If nothing comes up — or if they find a competitor's website instead — you've lost them.

Give them something to find. A clean, fast, professional website that confirms: yes, this is a real business, here's what they do, here's how to reach them.

That's all it takes. And at Aetherstack, that starts at ₹1,000.
